# Identification of Key Genes Associated with Lung Cancer Progression

## Overview

Bioinformatics-based transcriptomic analysis to identify key genes associated with lung cancer progression using RNA-Seq data from The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA).

This study focused on identifying differentially expressed genes (DEGs), exploring their biological significance through Gene Ontology (GO) and KEGG pathway enrichment analyses, and evaluating their potential as biomarkers for lung cancer progression.

Academic Thesis Project - MGM University

## Dataset

- Source: TCGA (The Cancer Genome Atlas)
- Cancer Types: LUAD & LUSC
- Type: RNA-Seq Gene Expression Data
- Samples: 20 Tumor + 20 Normal Samples

## Objectives

- Data Collection and Preprocessing
- Differential Gene Expression Analysis
- Functional Enrichment Analysis
- Prognostic Interpretation of Key Genes

## Methods

- RNA-Seq Data Processing
- Log2 Transformation
- Differential Gene Expression Analysis
- Gene Annotation
- GO Enrichment Analysis
- KEGG Pathway Analysis
- Data Visualization

## Functional Insights

### Gene Ontology (GO)

Enriched biological processes included:

- Regulation of Intracellular Signal Transduction
- Cell Population Proliferation
- DNA Damage Response
- Positive Regulation of Gene Expression
- Protein Modification Processes

### KEGG Pathways

Significantly enriched pathways included:

- Cell Cycle
- p53 Signaling Pathway
- PI3K-Akt Signaling Pathway
- Metabolic Reprogramming Pathways

## Visualizations

- Volcano Plot

## Key Findings

- Identified critical genes associated with lung cancer progression.
- Upregulated genes were linked to proliferation, metabolism, and mitotic progression.
- Downregulated genes were associated with stress response and transcriptional regulation.
- Functional enrichment analyses highlighted pathways involved in tumor growth and survival.
- Identified candidate biomarkers and potential therapeutic targets for lung cancer.
